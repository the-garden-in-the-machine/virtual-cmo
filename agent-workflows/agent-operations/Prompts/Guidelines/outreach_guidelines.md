# Outreach Guidelines

Use these principles to guide the style, structure, and tone of cold outreach drafts.

## 1. Tone & Voice
* **Quiet and Editorial:** Avoid generic sales jargon, hyperbole, or exclamation marks. Communication should feel premium, direct, and ink-on-paper minimalist.
* **Low Hype, High Specificity:** Never make claims like "revolutionary", "game-changing", or "the ultimate solution". Focus purely on the mechanics of what we automate (e.g., *"We automate the path from raw Figma frames and narration files to finished MP4 videos"*).
* **Respectful and Professional:** Establish credibility through clear sentence structures, proper formatting, and brevity. Value the reader's time by keeping the text under 150 words.

## 2. Personalization Strategy
* **Establish Common Ground:** If mutual connections are found via LinkedIn Sales Navigator, mention them early but naturally (e.g., *"I noticed we are both connected to [Mutual Name]..."*).
* **Target Role Relevance:** Adjust the pitch based on the prospect's title:
  * *Creative Directors:* Focus on saving designers' time, maintaining aesthetic consistency, and eliminating routine formatting tasks.
  * *CEOs / Founders:* Focus on bottlenecks, scaling output, and resource optimization.
  * *Heads of Production:* Focus on pipeline reliability, rendering speeds, and process automation.
* **Show, Don't Just Tell:** Reference their specific brand or projects to demonstrate we have reviewed their online presence and style guidelines.

## 3. Letter Structure
1. **Salutation:** Professional and simple (e.g., *Hi [Name],*).
2. **The Connection (Context):** A brief sentence introducing the reason for reaching out (mutual contact, a specific recent project they published, or industry focus).
3. **The Problem:** Address a common creative production bottleneck (e.g., spending hours formatting variants, burning subtitles, or resizing video grids).
4. **Our Solution (The Pitch):** A simple description of how we solve this at Emergentic using creative engineering pipelines (e.g., AppleScript, ExtendScript, Playwright).
5. **Call to Action (CTA):** A light, low-friction request to discuss their existing production flow (e.g., *"If you are experiencing similar bottlenecks in your publishing pipeline, I'd love to share how we set these up."*).
6. **Sign-off:** Simple (e.g., *Best,* or *Regards,* followed by name and title).
